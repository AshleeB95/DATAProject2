NYPD: Edenwald and neighboring communities see crime decrease by 64%

By: Ashlee Brown

Nov. 12, 2022
Parked police car lights shine blue. | Source: Pexels.

Although NYPD's 47th precinct experienced five murders in one week in Sept. 2021, police department historical data reports a 64% drop in crime since 1990.

The crime in this data includes, Murder, Rape, Robbery, Felony Assault, Burglary, Grand Larceny and G.L.A., also known as Grand Larceny Auto from Nov. 1990 - Nov. 2021.

Edenwald Council member Kevin C. Riley represents the 12th district and believes the drop in crime is a result of community support and partnership.

"It's important to give community members, for example, children, something to do or they'll find something to do. I believe in creating programs that interest today's members," says Riley. "It's important and absolutely okay to change with society."

Out of the seven listed crimes, only reports of Felony Assault have increased since 1990 - by 3.8%. There have also been less burglaries reported in the area; having decreased the most (85.3%).
